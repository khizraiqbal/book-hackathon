# Humanoid Robotics Book - Complete Course Specification

## Overview
This comprehensive course covers humanoid robotics from fundamentals to advanced simulation techniques. The content is designed for students and developers in Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics, providing both theoretical foundations and practical implementation skills.

## Course Structure

### Module 1: Introduction to Humanoid Robotics
Target: Beginners to intermediate students
Focus: Fundamental concepts, kinematics, and control systems
Audience: Students new to humanoid robotics

#### Chapter 1: Fundamentals of Humanoid Robotics
- History and development of humanoid robots
- Key components and subsystems
- Degrees of freedom and joint configurations
- Applications and use cases

#### Chapter 2: Kinematics and Mathematical Foundations
- Forward and inverse kinematics
- Denavit-Hartenberg parameters
- Coordinate systems and transformations
- Jacobian matrices and motion planning

#### Chapter 3: Control Systems and Basic Locomotion
- Control theory applications in robotics
- Balance and stability control
- Basic walking patterns and gait generation
- Sensor integration for feedback control

### Module 2: The Digital Twin (Gazebo & Unity)
Target: Students with basic robotics knowledge
Focus: Physics simulation, environment building, and sensor simulation
Audience: Students & developers in Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics

#### Chapter 1: Gazebo Fundamentals
- Digital twins, physics, gravity, and collision simulation
- Configuration and setup
- Integration with ROS 2

#### Chapter 2: Unity for Human-Robot Interaction
- Rendering, environments, and interaction logic
- High-fidelity visualization
- Integration with robotics frameworks

#### Chapter 3: Sensor Simulation
- LiDAR, depth cameras, IMUs, and data flow to ROS 2
- Realistic sensor modeling
- Data processing and validation
